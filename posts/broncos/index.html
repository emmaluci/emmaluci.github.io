Are the Denver Broncos the worst offensive-scoring team in the NFL?

Author

Emma Luci

Published

April 16, 2022

Denver Broncos fans are already looking forward to the 2023 season. Dedicated fans were caught blindly optimistic after the five-year contract binding Russell Wilson to the 2021 team last march was able to fill the team’s quarterback position. Yet, after signing Wilson under a  hefty $245 million, it seems there aren’t any easy solutions for the franchise following his rocky start.  

The recent addition of head coach Sean Payton is certain to stir up a new found hope once again in the city of Denver and for fans across the country. Though the off season is the perfect time to project the future for the franchise, it is also the most important time to reflect on the past dreadful seasons. Not only did the team struggle tremendously last season, but they had reportedly the worst-scoring offense, averaging at just 16.9 points for each game they played. The injury of back left tackle Garret Boles left a huge dent in their offensive line. Denver was also one of the worst sack rate teams in the NFL this last season ranking in at 30th overall among teams. Many fans pointed fingers at Russell Wilson, as he was ranked 25th statistically out of 32 quarterbacks with at least 300 drop backs and a completion percentage that was surprisingly above the anticipated -1.1 percent. The broncos have been unsuccessful with filling the quarterback position comfortably since the loss of Peyton Manning after the team’s Superbowl winning 2015 season.

Naturally, Broncos fans have a deep burning hatred for the Kansas City Chiefs that runs through our bloodstream. With that being said, it is utterly painful to see the visuals of the absolute downfall of my favorite football team. It doesn’t take a wild guess to see that this huge drop-off had something to do with the painful loss of Peyton Manning after we won the 2015 Superbowl.

After investigating multiple curiosities on the awful past performances of my favorite, I was slightly surprised to find out I was wrong when I had originally thought that this past season had to be the “historically worst” season for the franchise. It was surprising to look at how the Broncos lined up with the rest of the teams in the league. The hire of Sean Payton for $18 million per season as the newest head coach for the upcoming 2023 season is a new found spark of the dying flame that is my hope for the future of the Denver Broncos. I am interested to see what the return of Vance Joseph will bring to the team, as well as Joe Lombardi in charge of the offense. Well we all know the saying, so lets ride.
